## SECTION 2: AI DIAL CADENCE (Workflow 01)

**GHL Workflow:** 01 — AI Dial Sequence (double_call + pre-call SMS)
**Trigger:** Tag `new lead`
**Primary channel:** AI voice call via RizzDial webhook with `double_call: true`

### Architecture

```
DAY 1 (within business hours of opt-in)
  Minute 0:  Pre-call SMS sends
  Minute 1:  RizzDial webhook fires with double_call:true
             |
             Agent dials once
             |
             +- ANSWERED > qualify > outcome tag > STOP
             +- NO ANSWER > RizzDial auto-dials (native)
                            |
                            +- ANSWERED > outcome tag > STOP
                            +- NO ANSWER > tag ai-agent-no-answer > STOP for day

             NO VOICEMAIL LEFT on either attempt.

DAYS 2-14 (business days only, 9am-5pm [TZ] Mon-Fri)
  Once per day, same pattern:
    Pre-call SMS > (60s) > RizzDial webhook (double_call:true)

STOP CONDITIONS
  Booked / Transferred / Disqualified / DNC / Bad Number → STOP
  Callback-requested → PAUSE until time
  Day 14 reached → tag ai-agent-exhausted, STOP
```

### Why Back-to-Back Dialing (not voicemails)
The first ring often gets ignored. The second dial 90 seconds later catches the reader looking at their phone after the missed-call notification. Same phone-in-hand window, two chances to connect. Voicemails waste per-send cost and rarely convert — the double call is free and it works.

### Why a 14-Day Cap
The client's own removal automations should pull leads out earlier based on booking, journey-stage triggers, or other tags. Day 14 is a safety net so no lead stays in the dialer forever if something upstream misfires.

## SECTION 12: WHY THIS WORKS

> This is the most important section for client signoff. Clients trust what they understand.

### The Handoff Model
This playbook owns the dial layer (tags, cadence, pre-call SMS, chatbot). The client's existing automations own the journey layer (post-booking, email nurture, removal logic). The bridge between them is the tag schema in Section 1. When the agent ends a call, one tag fires. That tag tells the client's system what to do next.

### Why Double Call (Not Voicemails)
The first ring gets ignored. The second dial 90 seconds later catches the reader looking at their phone after the missed-call notification. Same phone-in-hand window, two chances to connect. Voicemails cost money per send and rarely get played. Double call is free and it works.

### Why Back-to-Back (Not a Blitz)
8 calls in one day with SMS retries between each feels like harassment and burns the relationship. 2 dials in a 3-minute window catches the moment without being obnoxious. Same conversion lift, dramatically less DNC risk.

### Why the 14-Day Cap
Your removal automations should pull leads out earlier based on booking, tag changes, or journey-stage logic. The cap is a safety net if something misfires — so Blake doesn't keep dialing them for 6 months. Day 14 soft-stops with a tag the client can review.

### Why Radical Specificity in Copy
Generic copy fails because it's written in a marketer's language. Every email and SMS in this playbook uses the client's actual numbers, named clients, specific dollar amounts, and real scenes. That's what moves prospects from skeptical to convinced.
