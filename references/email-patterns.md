# Email Copy Patterns -- Master Template Library

> Templates for every email touchpoint across all GHL workflows.
> Rules: No em dashes. Under 200 words. One CTA. Seinfeld style for nurture.
> Open with the hook, not "Hey {{first_name}}!"

---

## THE SEINFELD EMAIL FRAMEWORK

Named after how Seinfeld episodes seem random but always connect back.

### Structure Per Email:
1. **Hook** (1-2 sentences) -- Unexpected statement, story opener, or observation
2. **Story** (3-5 sentences) -- Short narrative that mirrors their situation
3. **Bridge** (1 sentence) -- Connect story to the offer naturally
4. **Soft CTA** (1 sentence) -- Casual, no pressure, woven into narrative

### Rules:
- Tell a short story (real or hypothetical)
- Connect it to their pain or desire
- End with casual CTA, not hard pitch
- Be entertaining enough they WANT to open the next one
- Leave open loops (reference next email)
- Never feel templated
- No corporate language
- No "Click here now!" CTAs

### What Kills Seinfeld Emails:
- "We're excited to announce..."
- No personality
- Hard CTAs with urgency they didn't earn
- No story element
- Feeling identical to every other email they get
- Too many paragraphs
- Starting with "Hey [Name]!"

### Example Skeleton (Med Spa):
**Subject:** The worst $200 I ever spent
**Hook:** I once paid $200 for a facial that made my skin worse.
**Story:** The esthetician used the same product on everyone. Didn't ask about my skin type, didn't check for sensitivities, just went through the motions. Two days later I looked like a tomato.
**Bridge:** That's what happens when businesses treat every customer the same. Your leads aren't all the same either.
**Soft CTA:** If you want to see how we personalize follow-up for every lead that walks through your door, grab a time here: [LINK]

### Example Skeleton (HVAC):
**Subject:** 47 missed calls last month
**Hook:** I pulled a report for an HVAC company last week. 47 inbound calls went to voicemail in one month.
**Story:** They had the leads. The ads were working. But nobody picked up the phone fast enough. By the time they called back, the homeowner already booked someone else.
**Bridge:** Speed wins in home services. Not ads, not branding. Speed.
**Soft CTA:** We built something that fixes this. 10 seconds from lead to call. Worth a look: [LINK]

### Example Skeleton (Coaching):
**Subject:** My client almost quit
**Hook:** Last Tuesday a client told me she was done.
**Story:** Six months building her course. Two launches. Seven sales total. She thought the problem was her content. It wasn't. Her content was solid. Her message was invisible. Nobody knew what she actually did or why they should care.
**Bridge:** We rebuilt her positioning in one session. Same course, different message.
**Soft CTA:** If your offer is good but nobody's buying, the problem isn't the offer. Reply "message" and I'll show you what I mean.

## 5 PERSUASION PILLARS (For All Nurture Content)

Use these as the emotional backbone for nurture emails. Rotate through them.

1. **Identity** -- "You are capable of more than the results you're getting."
2. **Cost of Inaction** -- "What happens if nothing changes for another year?"
3. **Opportunity** -- "There is revenue attached to fixing this."
4. **Authority** -- "This works because we've done it [X] times."
5. **Proof of Implementation** -- "People who do the work get results."

---

## 7 CORE EMAIL ANGLES (Reusable Across All Industries)

1. **The Invisible Cost** -- "You're spending $X/month on ads. 80% of those leads die in your inbox. That's the real cost."
2. **The Unused Value** -- "Your [leads/data/audience/skills] are either working for you or sitting there unused."
3. **The Authority** -- "Why try to figure this out from someone who hasn't done it at scale?"
4. **The Price Reframe** -- "This isn't expensive. Staying where you are is expensive."
5. **The Identity Shift** -- "You don't have a [lead gen/sales/marketing] problem. You have a [follow-up/messaging/system] problem."
6. **The Speed** -- "You don't need another year to figure this out. You need a system."
7. **The Call-Out** -- "Don't start if you're not going to commit. This only works for people who do the work."
